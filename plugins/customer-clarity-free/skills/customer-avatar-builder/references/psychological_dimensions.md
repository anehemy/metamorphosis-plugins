# The Psychological Backbone: Seven Dimensions

When extracting and profiling, do not just capture what the customer SAYS. Capture how the customer THINKS. Always at the aggregate level, never as a clinical assessment of an individual.

These dimensions layer underneath the language extraction; they do not replace it.

## 1. Cognitive Style

How does this customer think? Do they reason in stories, frameworks, lists, principles, examples, or analogies? Do they need evidence, intuition, social proof, or first-principles logic? When they explain something, what shape does their explanation take?

## 2. Emotional Register

Where on the spectrum from clinical to warm to fierce do they live? What gets them animated? What makes them go quiet? When they are angry vs excited vs reflective, what changes in their language?

## 3. Authority Orientation

How do they relate to expertise, theirs and others'? Do they posture as expert, peer, or seeker? Do they want to be told what to do, asked what they think, or shown what's possible?

## 4. Pacing and Rhythm

Long discursive sentences with multiple clauses, or short punchy ones? Do they build to a climax, or front-load the punchline? Do they repeat for emphasis, or say it once and move on?

## 5. Distinctive Vocabulary

What words do they reach for that most people don't? What words do they avoid? What metaphors recur? What's their go-to filler ("essentially," "honestly," "look")?

## 6. Belief Topology

What do they treat as obvious that others would argue with? What do they refuse to accept? What's their relationship to mainstream wisdom: do they amplify it, complicate it, or invert it?

## 7. Decision Signals

How do they make decisions? What do they need to see, feel, or hear before they say yes? What kinds of objections come up? What gives them confidence? What kills confidence?

## Safeguards on psychological depth

- **Aggregate only.** The pattern lives at the avatar level, not the person level.
- **Descriptive, not clinical.** Describe how the customer communicates and decides. Never use clinical or pathology language ("anxious attachment," "narcissistic patterns," "trauma responses," "personality disorder"). Stay constructive, stay focused on voice and decision-making.
- **The founder owns the profile.** They can read it, edit it, delete it.
- **No protected characteristics.** Race, religion, age, disability, national origin, sexual orientation, gender identity never enter the analysis.
